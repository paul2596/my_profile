May-August 2023:
Analyzed the Impact of Using Additional Weather Information on the Accuracy of Power Production Models for Offshore Wind Turbines
  • Worked collaboratively under the guidance of supervisors from National Innovation Center for Data, UK and Equinor, Norway.
  • Carried out an comprehensive analysis to study impact of additional atmospheric features and atmospheric stability parameter like Obukhov Length by combining data from the turbine and weather information from climate reanalysis data generated from numerical weather prediction models .
  • A directional analysis was carried out to study influence of features on power prediction when wind flows across land and across sea.
  • The trained models were then deployed using flask framework with python for backend and AngularJS to perform interactions at the front end.
